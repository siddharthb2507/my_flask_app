What are Indices?

Indices, in the context of remote sensing and image analysis, are mathematical algorithms or formulas applied to satellite or aerial imagery to extract specific information about the Earth's surface or atmosphere. These indices are derived from the spectral reflectance properties of different features on the Earth's surface, captured by remote sensing sensors.



Purpose of Indices

Feature Identification:

Indices help in identifying and distinguishing different features or phenomena on the Earth's surface, such as vegetation, water bodies, urban areas, and more.

Quantitative Analysis:

They provide quantitative information about various environmental parameters, such as vegetation health, water quality, soil moisture, and land surface temperature.

Monitoring and Assessment:

Indices enable the monitoring and assessment of changes in environmental conditions over time, such as deforestation, land degradation, urban expansion, and agricultural productivity.

Environmental Modeling:

They are used as input parameters in environmental models and simulations for predicting and understanding natural processes and phenomena.



Indices and Their Uses

Indices are mathematical calculations applied to satellite imagery to extract specific information. Here's why they are useful:

  • NDWI (Normalized Difference Water Index) - Used to identify water bodies
  • MNDWI (Modified Normalized Difference Water Index) - Similar to NDWI, with adjustments for urban areas
  • NDMI (Normalized Difference Moisture Index) - Indicates vegetation moisture content
  • LSWI (Land Surface Water Index) - Detects surface water on land
  • NDTI (Normalized DIfference Turbidity Index) - Measures water turbidity, useful for environmental monitoring
  • SAVI (Soil Adjusted Vegetation Index) - Separates soil from vegetation
  • DBSI (Dry Bare Soil Index) - Identifies bare soil areas, particularly useful in dry regions
  • BSI (Bare Soil Index) - Identifies bare soil areas
  • HBSI (Hybrid Bare Soil Index) - Combines BSI and SAVI for improved soil identification, especially in areas with mixed vegetation and soil
  • NDSI (Normalized Difference Soil Index) - Identifies bare soil and separates soil from vegetation
  • NDVI (Normalized Difference Vegetation Index) - Widely used measure of vegetation health, density, and greenness.
  • GNDVI (Green Normalized Difference Vegetation Index) - More sensitive to chlorophyll content than NDVI, useful for plant health later in the season.
  • NBR (Normalized Burn Ratio) - Helps assess burn severity by exploiting changes in near-infrared and shortwave infrared reflectance after fire.
  • OSAVI (Optimum Soil Adjusted Vegetation Index) - Similar to SAVI but with a dynamically adjusted soil adjustment factor.
  • NDRE (Normalized Difference Red Edge Vegetation Index) - Detects stress in vegetation earlier than NDVI due to its sensitivity to the red-edge region, crucial for early intervention.
  • RCVI (Red-edge Chlorophyll Vegetation Index) - Provides a more specific assessment of chlorophyll content in plants compared to NDVI, aiding in monitoring plant health and nutrient deficiencies.
